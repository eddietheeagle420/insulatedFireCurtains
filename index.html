Firescreen® Temperature (EI)

The Firescreen® Range - Brought to you by Hoefnagels Fire Safety

Firescreen® Temperature fire-resistant roller screens meet the important criteria: flame density to keep the element intact (E), limitation of thermal radiation of up to 1 kW/m2 (W) and thermal insulation of the temperature (I). With these roller screens you can, for example, realise an evacuation route or create an attack route for the fire brigade.

Firescreen® Temperature fire curtains are suitable for sealing off fire compartments and ensure that, at a temperature of 1000°C on the fire side, the rise on the non-fire side is only 140°C. Firescreen Temperature fire-resistant roller screens have an EI classification and are tested according to EN 1634-1 and EN 13501-1.

Optionally, we can supply both the Standard and P versions of this roller screen with a smoke-resistant function, Sa / S200, in accordance with the standard EN 1634-3.

Thermax Installations

Thermax are now proud to offer the Firescreen® range of insulated fire curtains.

These insulated fire curtains are tested in accordance with EN 1634-1 & EN 13501-1, and are available in multiple classifications varying from EI 30 up to EI 120. These curtains offer insulation without the use of additional sprinklers. We can also offer smoke seals to curtains up to EI 120 with either Sa or S200 rating. The Firescreen Temperature curtains are ideal when creating escape routes or alternatively an advance route for fire fighters when entering a building.

SPECIFICATION

Firescreen® Temperature fire curtains meet the important criteria: Flame containment in order to maintain integrity (E), they also limit heat radiation on the non-fire side to a maximum of 1 kW/ m2 (W). Thereby Firescreen Temperature fire curtains qualify for the thermal insulation criteria (I), which means that at a temperature of 1000 ° C on the fire side, these fire curtains ensure that the surface temperature increase on the non-fire side remains below 140 °C. All Firescreen Temperature fire curtains therefore have an EI classification tested according to EN 1634-1.
